The Evolution of Computers

World War II: The first electric programmable computer was developed by Thomas Flowers, an English engineer, who named it Colossus and was made to help decrypt German messages sent during the War.

1948: The first computer able to store programs was built by Tom Kilburn and designed by Frederic Williams at the University of Manchester, England. It was named the SSEM or Small-Scale Experimental Machine and the first electronically-stored program was executed on June 21 within the same year.

1953: IBM released its first commercial computer, the so called 701. It was produced and sold 19701 times, hence it being the first mass produced computer.

1964: The first desktop, named the Programma 101, was invented by Pier Giorgio Perotto, manufactured by Olivetti and sold about 44000 times, each costing $3200.

1975: The first so called portable computer was the IBM 5100, which was released in September of 1975. It had a 5 inch display, weighed a total of 55 pounds (24.9 Kg), included a PALM CPU clocked at 1.9 MHz and had 64 KB of RAM.

1976: Apple released its first Computer, the Apple I. It was developed by Steve Wozniak and included a 65092 8-bit processor and 4 kb of RAM, which could be upgraded to 8 kb or 48 kb. However the Apple I still needed a separate keyboard, power supply, display and case to be operational.

1977: Apple II was released by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak, which was capable of color graphics and included an audio cassette drive for storage.

1981: IBM released its first personal computer named IBM PC. It included a 8088 processor, 16 KB of RAM, which was upgradable to 256 KB, and it used the Microsoft Disk Operating System.

1984: Apple released the Macintosh, the first mass produced PC to use a Graphical User Interface (GUI). It included a 68000 CPU clocked at 8 MHz, 128 kB or 512 kB of RAM and a 9 inch display.

2003: The first 64-bit CPU was released by AMD, the AMD Athlon 64.
2006: Apple released the first MacBook Pro, which included a dual-core CPU by Intel, and the first iMac, which was also based on an Intel processor.

2011: The first Chromebook was announced and sold by Acer and Samsung, which ran on the Google Chrome Operating System.

2016: The first programmable quantum computer was built by Shantanu Debnath and his colleagues at the University of Maryland.
